**Full title**: Road Intelligence Platform — Financial Rebase
**Author**: Tony Jung
**Date**: April 10, 2026
**Purpose**: Update build plan financial projections with GPS-verified engine hours, telemetry-verified fuel rates, and confirmed UFA pricing.

## Summary

Reconciles the April 6 build plan's assumption-based projections against four sources of verified data: UFA $1.57/L lock-in (April 9), Titan GPS odometer hours (786 hrs/unit/yr, 42% below assumed 1,350), JD telemetry fuel rates (21.3 L/hr working, 4.4 L/hr idle), and CAT Fleet Utilization (18.2-22.7 L/hr). Revises annual grader fleet fuel from $583K down to ~$375K-$400K. Produces a deadhead-ratio sensitivity table and revises Year 1 savings from $65K-$75K to a range of $40K-$75K combined (fuel + productivity). Establishes the 5-year trajectory as a range ($345K-$535K cumulative) rather than a single point estimate ($530K), with the explicit instruction to not present revised financials until the GPS baseline produces the actual deadhead ratio.

## Key Facts

- **UFA diesel lock-in**: $1.57/L confirmed April 9, 2026 (was modeled at $1.60)
- **Fuel rate correction**: JD telemetry shows 21.3 L/hr working (vs original 18 L/hr assumption), 4.4 L/hr idle
- **Engine hours**: Titan GPS gives ~786 hrs/unit (42% below original 1,350)
- **GPS coverage**: 9 of 14 graders (36% missing vs plan assumption of full coverage)
- **7-unit tracked fleet fuel**: 240,407 L / $377,438/yr
- **Fleet-wide projection**: ~$375K-$400K/yr
- **Revised Year 1 savings**: $40K-$75K combined (conservative: $22.5K at 20% deadhead × 30% reduction; optimistic: $56K at 30% deadhead × 50% reduction)
- **Revised 5-year cumulative**: $345K-$535K (was single point $530K)

## Decision Captured

Do not present revised financials to Ryan or Council until the GPS baseline is complete. The four numbers GPS produces (actual deadhead ratio, fuel split, route overlap, seasonal pattern) replace all assumption layers above. The baseline number is what goes to Council.
