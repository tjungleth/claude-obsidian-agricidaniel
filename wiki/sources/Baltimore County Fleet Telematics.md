# Source: Baltimore County Fleet Telematics

## Summary

Baltimore County, MD Department of Public Works deployed web-based GPS fleet tracking and dispatching for snow removal and general public works operations. The system enabled supervisors to view real-time vehicle locations and dispatch the closest vehicle to emergencies or unscheduled work.

## Key Results

- **165,000 fewer miles driven** in the measurement period
- **$79,000 fuel cost reduction** from more efficient routing
- **$354,000 total productivity savings** in six months
- **3-month ROI**: equipment costs recouped within the first quarter
- Supervisors gained real-time dispatch visibility (GPS map of all vehicles)

## Relevance to Road Intelligence Platform

This is the closest published case study to Lethbridge County's Road Intelligence Platform. Both involve:
- County-level public works with mixed fleet (snow, grading, general maintenance)
- GPS-based visibility as the foundation for optimization
- Dispatch efficiency as a primary savings mechanism

The $354K/6-month figure translates to ~$708K annualized for a presumably larger fleet than Lethbridge County's 14 graders, but the proportional savings pattern (fuel + productivity + dispatch efficiency) is consistent with the RIP's $80K-$150K fully loaded projection.
