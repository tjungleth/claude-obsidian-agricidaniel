# Source: TAC Canadian Gravel Road Survey

## Summary

2018 Transportation Association of Canada paper surveying 97 Canadian municipalities on gravel road maintenance practices, representing approximately 40,000 km of gravel roads.

## Key Findings

- **60% of Canada's public road network is unpaved** — gravel road management is a national-scale problem, not a niche concern.
- **97 municipalities surveyed** across Canada, collectively managing ~40,000 km of gravel roads.
- Municipalities spend millions annually on gravel road maintenance and rehabilitation.
- Most municipalities lack a comprehensive road management system that includes gravel roads — paved road management systems exist, but gravel is often managed ad hoc.
- A Gravel Road Management System (GRMS) that includes condition assessment, prioritization, and budget planning could substantially improve operating and capital budget management.

## Relevance to Road Intelligence Platform

The RIP is effectively building what this paper recommends: a data-driven gravel road management system. The survey confirms that:
1. The problem Lethbridge County faces (ad hoc gravel road management) is widespread across Canadian municipalities.
2. No published case study exists of a municipality that has fully implemented GPS-based route optimization for graders — the RIP is breaking new ground.
3. The [[Demand-Driven Scheduling]] concept (priority-based grading frequency) aligns with the paper's recommendation for condition-based maintenance planning.
