# Treatment Optimization

> [!NOTE] Concept stage
> This workstream is identified in the Tier 3 assessment but has no active sprint work. It compounds on top of Tier 1 and Tier 2 results.

## Summary

Treatment optimization allocates grading, dust control, and road stabilization budgets segment by segment to maximize road integrity per dollar spent. Instead of applying uniform treatment across the network, each segment gets the combination of grading frequency, dust control, and stabilization that maximizes its durability given its traffic load and condition.

The gold standard KPI applies at the network level: given a fixed budget, how do you maximize the total road-kilometres maintained at acceptable integrity across the entire gravel network?

Estimated value: capital allocation analysis (Report 2, Tier 3). No dollar figure assigned. The value is strategic: better allocation of existing budgets rather than new spending.

## Mechanism

1. Build a per-segment cost model: what does it cost to maintain each road segment at an acceptable condition level, considering grading frequency, dust control, and stabilization treatments?
2. Optimize the allocation: given a fixed total budget, which segments get more investment and which get less, to maximize network-wide road integrity duration?
3. Identify segments where stabilization (e.g., calcium chloride, asphalt emulsion) is more cost-effective than frequent grading.

This is a capital planning exercise that builds on data from [[Dust Control Coordination]], [[concepts/Demand-Driven Scheduling|Demand-Driven Scheduling]], and the [[Grader Performance Score]].

## Connection to Road Integrity

Some segments degrade faster than others regardless of grading quality: high-traffic intersections, roads with poor subgrade, segments exposed to heavy runoff. Treatment optimization asks where the county should invest in stabilization versus frequent grading to get the most durable road network per budget dollar. The answer requires both the cost data (from Tier 1/2 workstreams) and the durability data (from [[Weather-Responsive Scheduling]] and [[Grader Performance Score]]).
