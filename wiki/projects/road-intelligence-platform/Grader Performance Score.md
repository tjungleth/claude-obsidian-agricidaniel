# Grader Performance Score

> [!NOTE] Concept stage
> This workstream is identified in the Tier 3 assessment but has no active sprint work. It compounds on top of Tier 1 and Tier 2 results.

## Summary

The Grader Performance Score identifies which operators produce the most durable grading results and supports peer learning across the fleet. The score measures the gold standard KPI: **how long a road maintains its integrity after grading**.

Estimated value: compounds over seasons (Report 2, Tier 3). No dollar figure assigned. The compounding mechanism: as lower-performing operators learn from higher-performing ones, the average quality of grading across the fleet rises, reducing the total number of grading passes needed to maintain the network.

## Mechanism

1. Correlate operator identity with post-grading road condition durability (how many days or weeks until the segment needs re-grading).
2. Control for confounding factors: road type, traffic volume, weather at time of grading, equipment condition.
3. Produce a per-operator score that isolates technique from circumstances.
4. Share results (sensitively) to enable peer learning: what are high-scoring operators doing differently (blade angle, speed, number of passes, moisture timing)?

The score requires data that doesn't fully exist yet: post-grading road condition tracking over time. The [[Fleet Efficiency Dashboard]] provides the operational metrics foundation; the Performance Score extends it to quality-of-outcome measurement.

## Connection to Road Integrity

Current metrics ([[Deadhead Ratio]], fuel consumption, grading hours) measure the efficiency of getting to the road. The Performance Score measures the quality of what happens when the grader gets there. Two operators can have identical deadhead ratios but produce grading that lasts very different amounts of time. This workstream closes that visibility gap.
